What is Data?

Data is a collection of facts, numbers, or text that can be processed by a computer. Let's look at real-world examples.

Student Marks

Scores, attendance, and grades used to predict student performance.

Online Shopping

Click rates, purchase history, and cart items used for recommendations.

Healthcare

Patient records, blood pressure, and lab results for diagnosing diseases.

Why Raw Data is a Problem

Real-world data is often incomplete, inconsistent, and contains errors. Models cannot learn from bad data. (Garbage In, Garbage Out).

Handling Missing Values

  • MCAR (Missing Completely at Random): No pattern to the missing data.
  • MAR (Missing at Random): Missingness depends on other observed data.
  • MNAR (Missing Not at Random): Missingness depends on the unobserved data itself.
